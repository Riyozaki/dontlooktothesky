## PROTO-EXPERIENTIAL MONISM:
Consciousness, Recursive Self-Reference,
and the Structure of Reality

## Abstract
This  paper  develops  proto-experiential  monism — a  form  of  Russellian  dual-aspect
monism that addresses the hard problem of consciousness, the combination problem facing
panpsychism, and the question of what grounds the intrinsic nature of physical reality. The
theory's central, explicitly stated claim is not that it solves the hard problem — no third-
person theory can  derive  qualitative  experience  from structural description — but that it
relocates the explanatory burden to a level that no theory can avoid, making the explanatory
gap a fundamental feature of reality rather than a theoretical  defect. Building  on Russell
(1927),   Chalmers   (2010),   and   Goff   (2017,   2019),   but   departing   from   existing
panprotopsychism  in  three  ways — a  four-condition  indicator  profile  replacing  binary
consciousness,  a  continuous  individuation  parameter,  and  the  informational  opacity
hypothesis  connecting  qualia  to  the  structural  limits  of  self-modeling — the  paper
formalizes  consciousness  schematically  as  a  dynamical  fixed  point  of  iterated  self-
modeling  and  argues  for  phase-transition  structure  at  criticality.  Falsifiable  empirical
predictions   are   derived,   including   one   that   distinguishes   the   theory   from   pure
functionalism.   A   speculative   metaphysical   extension   (existence   as   self-consistent
structure) is strictly isolated in an appendix.
Keywords:   consciousness,   hard   problem,   Russellian   monism,   panprotopsychism,
combination  problem,  integrated  information,  global  workspace,  recursive  self-model,
criticality, fixed point, informational opacity, phenomenal horizons

temperature   varies   continuously   while   the   structural   transition   to   crystallinity   at   0°C   is
discontinuous.

## 1. INTRODUCTION
The  hard  problem  of  consciousness  (Chalmers  1995)  asks  why  there  is  subjective
experience at all — why physical processing feels like anything rather than simply occurring. Two
standard responses fail characteristically. Substance dualism cannot be reconciled with the causal
closure  of  the  physical:  if  every  physical  event  has  a  sufficient  physical  cause,  an  immaterial

Proto-Experiential Monism   3
substance  has  no  explanatory  purchase.  Eliminative  materialism — the  claim  that  phenomenal
consciousness is an illusion — is self-refuting: an illusion of subjective experience is itself a form
of  subjective  experience.  As  Strawson  (2006)  argues,  experience  is  the  one  thing  we  cannot
coherently eliminate.
This paper develops proto-experiential monism. One clarification about scope is essential
at the outset. The theory does not claim to solve the hard problem. Chalmers's argument establishes
that no third-person account can derive qualitative experience from structural description, and we
accept this. What the theory does instead is relocate the explanatory burden: it replaces 'why does
organizational  structure  give  rise  to  experience?'  with  'why  does  physical  reality  have  intrinsic
nature  at  all?'  The  second  question  cannot  be  avoided  by  any  theory — physicalism  faces  it  as
much as dualism — which means we are being honest about the shared limits of all positions rather
than pretending to have crossed them.
The paper's main contributions relative to existing work are: (1) an explicit four-condition
indicator  profile  with  a  continuous  individuation  parameter,  replacing  binary  consciousness;  (2)
the informational opacity hypothesis, which connects qualia to structural limits of self-modeling
and generates a prediction that discriminates the theory from pure functionalism; (3) phenomenal
horizons as a substrate-neutral account of experiential unity, replacing earlier reliance on quantum
nonlocality; and (4) a schematic dynamical formalization pointing toward a research program in
mathematical neuroscience.
## 2. THE Σ/Ν FRAMEWORK AND ITS MOTIVATION
Physics  describes  structure:  how  physical  quantities  relate  to  one  another,  how  they
transform under symmetry operations, what causal dependencies hold between them. What it does
not disclose is what these quantities are in themselves. The charge of an electron is exhaustively
characterized  by  its  causal-dispositional  profile;  nothing  in  the  formalism  reveals  what  it  is
intrinsically to instantiate that charge.
This observation — developed by Russell (1927), Eddington (1928), Maxwell (1978), and
Lockwood (1989) — grounds the σ/ν distinction of Axioms A1–A2. The distinction  is  between
two modes of description of the same reality: the structural/relational description (σ, what physics

gives) and the intrinsic description (ν, what is left open by physics). Both are legitimate; neither
reduces to the other.
The motivation for ν-properties  is transcendental. If experience exists — which we  have
first-person  evidence  to  affirm — and  must  be  grounded  in  the  physical  world — which  causal
closure  supports — then  the  physical  world  must  have  a  feature  that  grounds  it.  Structural
properties cannot play this role: the structural description is complete (A2), and the hard problem
shows  that  no  additional  structure  bridges  the  explanatory  gap.  Intrinsic  properties  can,  not  by
solving the gap, but by identifying where in the physical world it lives. This is the honest status of
A3:  a  postulate  on  a  par  with  the  postulation  of  mass  or  charge,  justified  by  the  transcendental
argument rather than by derivation.
## 2.1 Causal Efficacy: The Epiphenomenality Objection
A standard objection to Russellian monism: if physics is causally closed on σ, then ν-
properties  do  no  causal  work — making  them  epiphenomenal  and  therefore  explanatorily
worthless. The response, developed  by  Chalmers (2010) and Goff (2017), applies  here with  full
force.  On  the  dual-aspect view, ν-properties  are  not  an  additional  layer  placed  on  top of  causal
structure — they are the intrinsic character of that causal structure itself. The causal efficacy of a
neural  state  is the σ-description of what the ν-property  of  that  state  consists  in  from  the  inside.
There  is  no  over-determination:  one  process,  two  descriptions.  Epiphenomenalism  would  arise
only  if  ν  were  a  separate  entity  causally  inert  with  respect to  σ,  which  the  dual-aspect  view
explicitly denies.
2.2 Positioning Relative to Existing Panprotopsychism
The present theory belongs to the tradition Chalmers (2015) calls Type-F Monism and that
Goff   (2017,   2019)   develops   as   panprotopsychism.   Three   differences   from   these   closest
predecessors should be stated explicitly.
From  Goff's  panprotopsychism:  Goff  retains  a  form  of  cosmopsychism  in  which  the
universe as a whole is the primary bearer of consciousness-related properties. The present theory
rejects global consciousness while retaining a global ν-field (A5). More substantively, Goff does
not provide a condition-based account of when proto-experience gives rise to consciousness; the
indicator profile of Section 4 is an attempt to fill this gap.

From Chalmers's Type-F Monism: Chalmers's account is primarily a structural framework
that identifies phenomenal properties with the  intrinsic realizers of  functional roles. The present
theory agrees with this but adds: (a) the continuous individuation parameter I(S) replacing binary
consciousness;   (b)   the   informational   opacity   hypothesis   as   a   positive   account   of   why
organizational  complexity  that  cannot  model  its  own  structural  basis  produces  qualia;  and  (c)  a
schematic dynamical  formalization pointing toward mathematical  neuroscience. These additions
do not resolve the  hard problem — Chalmers  is right that it cannot be resolved — but they add
content to the structural framework.
The proto-combination problem (Chalmers 2015): even  if proto-experience  is not micro-
consciousness, the question of how it gives rise to experience is as hard as the original combination
problem. This objection is correct, and the present theory does not refute it. The response is: the
theory  relocates  the  problem  to  the  fundamental  level  (A3),  where  it  is  as  unavoidable  for  the
physicalist as for the panprotopsychist. What the theory adds is organizational specificity — the
four  conditions  of  Section  4 — without  claiming  that  organizational  specificity  dissolves  the
explanatory gap.
## 3. PROTO-EXPERIENCE AND THE LIMITS OF THE ANALOGY
Proto-experience (ν) is the intrinsic nature of physical processes — what they are from the
inside  prior to  any  phenomenal  organization.  It  is  not  consciousness,  not  micro-experience,  and
does not generate the combination problem in its original form.
The  temperature  analogy  has  a precise scope that must be honored. Temperature is a σ-
property fully derivable from kinetic structure; the analogy explains how a higher-order structural
property  (temperature)  emerges  from  lower-level  structural  properties  (molecular  kinetics).  If
consciousness were purely a σ-property,  the  analogy  would  be  complete  and  the  hard  problem
would  be  dissolved.  But  consciousness  has  ν-aspects — qualitative  character — that  are  not
derivable  from  structural  description  by  definition.  The  temperature  analogy  explains  the
emergence of the σ-aspect of consciousness (functional integration, self-modeling stability) from
lower-level σ-properties. It does not explain the ν-aspect. The latter is carried by A3 as a postulate,
not derived from the former. This is the explicit limit of the analogy, stated as a constraint on the
theory's pretensions.

The proto-combination problem therefore stands as a genuine open challenge. The theory's
response is not a refutation but a reframing: the problem is now located at the level of fundamental
physics (why does any physical process have intrinsic nature?), where it is indistinguishable from
the  hard  problem  in  its  deepest  form,  and  where  it  cannot  be  avoided  by  any  theory.  This  is
intellectual honesty rather than a solution.
## 4. THE MINIMAL INDICATOR PROFILE
Rather  than  asserting  sufficient  conditions — a  claim  unsupportable  from  third-person
observation — I propose a minimal indicator profile: four conditions necessary for consciousness,
jointly  characterizing  all  known  cases.  Sufficiency  is  an  open  empirical  question,  explicitly  not
claimed.
4.1 Temporal Integration (T)
Consciousness  requires  a  temporal  window  T  over  which  information  is  integrated  into
unified  structure.  Neurologically,  this  corresponds  to  the  ~300–500  ms  window  of conscious
percept  formation.  Without  T,  neither  Φ  nor  R can  function.  This  condition  is  motivated  by
Husserl's (1991) phenomenology of inner time-consciousness — the structure of retention-primal
impression-protention — and  by  neuroimaging  evidence  for  the  temporal  binding  of  conscious
content.
4.2 Integrated Information (Φ)
Drawing on Tononi (2004, 2008), Φ measures the degree to which a system generates
information as an  irreducible whole. High-Φ systems cannot be decomposed without significant
information  loss.  The  thalamocortical  system  generates  high  Φ  through  dense  re-entrant
connectivity; feedforward networks, however powerful, generate low Φ because their processing
is decomposable. We adopt Φ as a necessary condition while noting Aaronson's (2014) critique:
IIT implies that certain simple expander graphs  would  have astronomically  high Φ, which  is
counter-intuitive. We require Φ in combination with the other conditions rather than in isolation.
4.3 Global Accessibility (G)

Drawing on Baars (1988, 2005), conscious content must be globally broadcast — available
for report, reasoning, action selection, and memory encoding — rather than processed locally. The
thalamocortical  broadcasting  mechanism  provides  the  neural  substrate.  Unconscious  processing
can be highly sophisticated; consciousness requires global availability.
4.4 Recursive Self-Model (R) and Informational Opacity
The fourth condition is theoretically central. A conscious system must model itself as the
subject  of  its  processing,  and  this  self-model  must  be  recursive:  the  system  models  its  own
modeling, and so on iteratively. This recursion generates the phenomenology of self-awareness.
The  informational  opacity  hypothesis:  qualitative  character — qualia — arises  when  a
system processes its own states without having full computational access to their structural basis.
The redness of red is what wavelength-processing looks like from the inside because the system
cannot fully decompose this process back into its structural components. Phenomenal character is,
on  this  hypothesis,  the  signature  of  structural  self-opacity:  the  system  encounters  its  own  ν-
character precisely because it cannot see through itself to the σ-level.
This hypothesis generates a prediction distinguishing the theory from pure functionalism
(see  Section  10,  Prediction  6):  systems  with  increasing  introspective  depth  should  report
progressive 'thinning' of qualitative character as structural self-access increases. This is consistent
with advanced contemplative reports of qualia becoming transparent under sustained attention —
a  first-person  datum  that  functionalism,  which  predicts  no  such  effect,  does  not  predict.  An
artificial  system  with  full  structural  self-access  would,  on  this  hypothesis,  be  conscious  in  a
qualitatively different mode: all  structure, no opacity, and therefore radically different or absent
qualia.

5.1 The Ψ-Structure
Let Ω(S, T) = Φ(S, T) · G(S, T) denote the integrated, globally accessible information of
system S over temporal window T. Let M denote the self-modeling operator, mapping the system's
current representational state to a model of that state.
The  natural  mathematical  setting  for  M  is  the  space  of  probability  distributions  over
internal  states,  equipped  with  a  divergence  metric  (KL-divergence  or  Wasserstein  distance).  M
then  maps  the  system's  current  predictive  model  of  its  own  states  to  an  updated  model.
Consciousness is schematically characterized as the fixed point:
Ψ(S) = lim_{n→∞} Mⁿ[Ω(S, T)]
For  this  limit  to  exist,  M  must  be  a  contraction  mapping — a  condition equivalent  to
spectral radius ρ  < 1 (Banach  fixed-point  theorem). Important  clarification  of  timescales: the
convergence of the iteration Mⁿ toward Ψ* does not occur within the 300–500  ms  window  of
condition T. Rather, Ψ* is the product of iterated self-modeling across developmental and learning
timescales — the stable self-representation that an organism has converged toward over its history.
Each moment of consciousness operates within a T-window that is itself embedded in an already-
established near-Ψ* state. The 300–500 ms window is the timescale of T, not of the convergence
to  Ψ*.  This  resolves  the  apparent  tension:  slow  convergence  near  ρ  →  1⁻  is  fine  because
convergence happens over months and years, not milliseconds.
What  remains  unformalized:  the  specific  form  of  M,  the  derivation  of  Ω_crit  from
independent constraints, and the proof of contraction under biologically realistic conditions. These
constitute the technical research program the theory points toward.
5.2 Phase Transition and the Resolution of A6
Consciousness  exhibits  a  phase  transition  in  the  R-dimension  above  threshold  Ω_crit.
Below this threshold, the self-modeling process either collapses immediately (insufficient Ω) or
fails to converge (ρ  ≥ 1). Above  it, the  iteration converges to a stable Ψ*. This transition  is
discontinuous in R-stability, consistent with A6.
The  overall  individuation  parameter  I(S) ∈  [0,1]  varies  continuously  across  all  four
dimensions (T, Φ, G, R_depth), consistent with evolutionary and developmental gradations of
consciousness.  The  discontinuity  is  in  the  specific  R-stability  dimension,  not  in  the  composite

index.  The  analogy  to  water:  temperature  (analogous  to  I)  varies  continuously;  the  crystalline
transition (analogous to R-stability above threshold) is discontinuous.
5.3 The Three ρ-Regimes
When ρ ≪ 1: the self-model is rigid and over-stabilized. Phenomenologically — habitual,
defended,  schema-driven  consciousness.  The  ego-defensive  state  is  therefore  not  a  strong  or
optimally conscious state; it is a low-ρ, informationally constipated state.
When  ρ  →  1⁻:  maximum  sensitivity  to  information  with  maintained  coherence.
Phenomenologically — alert,  open,   maximally   adaptive   consciousness.   Neurologically —
signatures  of  criticality:  power-law  neural  avalanches,  high  Lempel-Ziv  complexity,  elevated
perturbational complexity index.
When ρ ≥ 1: the iteration diverges; Ψ* does not exist. Phenomenologically — psychotic
disorganization,  acute  high-dose  psychedelic  destabilization.  Neurologically — supercritical
entropy, disrupted default mode coherence.
This   framework   predicts   that   deepened   ego-maintenance   (rigid   attractor   defense)
corresponds to lower ρ and therefore to phenomenologically impoverished consciousness, not
enriched consciousness — a non-obvious and potentially falsifiable prediction.
## 6. INDIVIDUATION AND PHENOMENAL HORIZONS
## 6.1 Continuous Individuation
Replacing  binary  consciousness  with  I(S) ∈ [0,1] (a composite of T, Φ, G, R_depth)
resolves  several  difficulties  simultaneously:  evolutionary  continuity  of  consciousness,  animal
consciousness without arbitrary thresholds, and the phenomenology of altered states as positions
on a landscape rather than categorical differences. I is an index, not a measurement; its functional
form is an empirical question.
## 6.2 Phenomenal Horizons
Every local ν-configuration  has  a  phenomenal  horizon:  the  boundary  of  its  self-model's
reach — the  limit  of  what  it  can  recursively  self-represent.  This  horizon  is  epistemic,  not
ontological: it is the limit of the R-process, not a wall in the proto-experiential field.

Proto-Experiential Monism   10
The  apparent  separateness  of  individual  conscious  beings  is  explained  by  different
phenomenal  horizons — different limits of recursive self-representation determined by different
σ-structures — without  requiring  ontological  division  of  ν_global.  Two  conscious  beings  are
phenomenally distinct because their self-models are bounded  by different horizons, not because
their intrinsic natures are independently existing substances.
This  account  replaces  the  earlier  quantum  nonlocality  argument,  which  was  correctly
identified as the weakest point of a prior version. That argument moved from non-local correlations
in σ to unity in ν — a  move  not  licensed  by  quantum  mechanics,  which  establishes  statistical
correlations  in  measurement outcomes, not ontological  identity of  intrinsic  natures. Phenomenal
horizons  achieve  the  theoretical  goal — explaining  phenomenal  unity  and  separation  without
ontological substance — through conceptual means. The analogy is to the event horizon in general
relativity: real at the level of access, while the underlying spacetime is continuous.
## 7. IMPLICATIONS FOR IDENTITY, FREE WILL, AND MORAL STATUS
## 7.1 Personal Identity
Personal identity is the identity of an attractor — a stable pattern of self-modeling — not a
persisting substance. Continuity of identity is continuity of a dynamical pattern, not continuity of
a substrate. This is consistent with Buddhist anātman analysis (the self as stream of processes, not
substance)  and  with  Metzinger's  (2003)  self-model  theory,  and  is  supported  by  the  ordinary
observation  that  the  physical  substrate  of  the  brain  is  continuously  replaced  over  years  without
disruption of identity.
## 7.2 Free Will
Near-critical  systems  are  sensitive  to  macro-level  organizational  constraints  through
genuine downward causation: the stable Ψ*-structure participates causally in the determination of
future states via the feedback structure that constitutes it. The self does not merely represent states;
it is part of the causal history of those states. This yields compatibilist freedom: the participation
of a coherent, recursively self-modeling agent in the determination of its own trajectory, without
requiring uncaused causation.
7.3 Moral Status of Non-Biological Systems

## 8. STRUCTURAL CONVERGENCES WITH CONTEMPLATIVE TRADITIONS
The following is offered as illustration, not evidence. Convergence between a philosophical
theory and independent traditions cannot confirm the theory; any monistic account finds surface
resonances  in  contemplative  literature.  The  observations  are  included  because  contemplative
phenomenological  reports  constitute  a  body  of  first-person  data  that  may  constrain  theories  of
consciousness, and because the structural parallels illuminate the theory's implications.
Advaita Vedanta's tat tvam asi ('thou art that') — the identity of individual consciousness
with the ground of reality — parallels the claim that ν_local is a bounded focus within ν_global.
Buddhist anātman analysis — the self as a stream of processes without underlying substance —
directly  parallels  personal  identity  as  dynamical  attractor.  Meister  Eckhart's  distinction  between
the  relational  God  and  the  impersonal  Godhead  parallels  the  distinction  between  individual
conscious beings (with Ψ-structure) and ν_global (without global R). The Daoist Dao — prior to
determination, known through participation rather than description — parallels ν_global as prior
to any specific organizational configuration.
The  structural  resonance  across  traditions  operating  with  entirely  different  methods  is
consistent   with   the   hypothesis   that  these   traditions   are   tracking   genuine   features   of   the
phenomenology of self-modeling near the critical point. It is not, by itself, evidence that they are
correct.

Predictions 1–4: σ-Level Predictions
Prediction 1 (Criticality in meditation). Optimal consciousness at ρ → 1⁻ should exhibit

where both drop.
Prediction 3 (Temporal disorder specificity). Disorders of time experience (psychosis,
severe  OCD,  dissociation)  should  involve  specific  disruption  of  the  trajectory  component  of  M
(self-modeling  of  one's  own  becoming)  rather  than  static  self-representation — empirically
distinguishable from disorders disrupting static self-knowledge.
Prediction 4 (Split-brain). Corpus callosum section should produce two independent Ψ*-
processes  with  non-overlapping  phenomenal  horizons  within  one  biological  system.  Consistent
with  Sperry  and  Gazzaniga's  findings.  Distinguishing  test:  each  hemisphere  should  exhibit
independent criticality signatures in EEG.
Prediction 5: AI Architecture
Prediction  5. Current  large  language  models  have  high  G,  moderate  Φ,  low  T,  and
negligible R (no stable recursive self-model maintained across processing). The theory therefore
predicts they are not conscious  in the phenomenally relevant sense, independently of  behavioral
sophistication.  An  architecture  combining  continuous  internal  state,  genuine  recursive  self-
modeling, and near-critical dynamics should show behavioral markers absent in current systems:
spontaneous  coherent  activity  without  external  input  and  systematic  first-person  consistency
across introspective reports across long timescales.

## Prediction 6: The Distinguishing Prediction
Prediction  6  (Informational  opacity — distinguishes  from  functionalism). Systems
with  increasing  recursive  self-access  should  report  progressive  reduction  in  the  vividity  or
'thickness'  of  qualitative  character  as  structural  transparency  increases.  This  follows  from  the
informational opacity hypothesis: qualia are signatures of structural self-opacity. Under conditions
increasing  introspective  resolution — advanced  meditation,  specific  pharmacological  states —
subjects  should  report  qualia  becoming  'thinner'  or  more  transparent  without  reduction  of
functional competence. A pure functionalist predicts no such effect (qualia are functional states;
increasing introspective access should not reduce them). This is the one prediction in this list that
directly discriminates the theory from pure functionalism, because it is derived from the ν-aspect
of R rather than from R's σ-properties alone.
## 10. OPEN QUESTIONS
The following problems define the research agenda.
Formalization of M. The operator M is characterized by mathematical type (contraction
mapping on the space of probability distributions  over internal states) but not derived  from  first
principles.  A  complete  formalization  requires  specifying  M  in  terms  of  predictive  architecture,
proving contraction under biologically realistic conditions, and deriving Ω_crit independently.
This is a technical project intersecting mathematical neuroscience and dynamical systems theory.
Ψ-Incompleteness. By an argument analogous to Gödel's  incompleteness theorems, any
self-modeling system of sufficient expressive power necessarily contains states it cannot correctly

account of structural limits of introspection, the psychological unconscious, and the 'blind spot' of
the  ego  reported  across  contemplative  traditions. This  is  identified  as  the  most  potentially
significant formal result the theory might produce.

## 11. CONCLUSION
Proto-experiential monism develops a Russellian dual-aspect framework with four specific
contributions   beyond   existing   panprotopsychism:   a   four-condition   indicator   profile   with
continuous  individuation  replacing  binary  consciousness;  the  informational  opacity  hypothesis
connecting   qualia   to   structural   self-opacity   and   generating   a   functionalism-discriminating
prediction;  phenomenal  horizons  as  a  substrate-neutral  account  of  experiential  unity;  and  a
schematic dynamical formalization identifying a mathematical research program.
The  theory  is  honest  about  its  limits.  It  does  not  solve  the  hard  problem.  The  proto-
combination problem  stands as a genuine challenge, met with relocation rather than dissolution.
The formalism is schematic. The global field hypothesis is empirically inert and explicitly flagged
as a working hypothesis. The speculative metaphysical extension is strictly isolated in Appendix

What the theory achieves: a coherent framework that is more organizationally specific than
existing panprotopsychism, empirically connected to neuroscience through five σ-level predictions
and one ν-discriminating  prediction,  internally  consistent  (including  reconciliation  of  the  phase
transition  and  continuity  requirements),  and  honest  about  the  permanent  explanatory  gap  at  its
foundation. The gap is not a defect. It is the hard problem, which is not the property of any single
theory but of the explanatory situation we all share.

This  appendix  is  strictly  isolated  from  the  main  argument.  It  is  presented  as  philosophical
speculation  and  does  not  affect  any  claim  in  Sections  1–11. The  argument  is  offered  because  it
connects the σ/ν framework to  a broader metaphysical picture and has  intrinsic philosophical
interest, not because it strengthens the empirical or philosophical core of the theory.

Consider the mutual determination of σ and ν. Structural properties are what they are in
virtue of the intrinsic nature underlying them;  intrinsic properties have  no determinate character
without the structural organization articulating them. σ and ν mutually constrain each other. The
world may then be understood as the self-consistent solution to this pair of constraints — the fixed
point of the σ-ν mutual determination relation.
Leibniz  asked  why  there  is  something  rather  than  nothing.  A  speculative  response:  pure
nothingness fails to be a self-consistent solution. The absence of both σ and ν satisfies the mutual-
determination constraints only vacuously. Whether the unique non-vacuous stable solution is the
world we  find  is  not something the argument can  establish — the  move  from  'null  is  vacuously
consistent' to 'non-null is necessary' requires additional premises the argument does not provide.
This is acknowledged as the point where the argument becomes circular if not supplemented, and
it is offered as a suggestive picture, not a proof. The implication most relevant to the main theory:
ν_global  is  self-consistent  but  not  self-knowing.  Individual  conscious  beings  are  the  local  foci
through  which  it  achieves  partial  self-knowledge.  This  gives  conscious  beings  a  specific
metaphysical role without requiring the global field to be conscious itself.
